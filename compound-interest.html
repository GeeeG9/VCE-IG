Australian Rate Snapshot

Real-world reference for class discussion, based on publicly listed Australian rates available in April 2026. These are examples only, and actual rates vary by bank, balance, fees, conditions, loan type, and credit profile.

Cash saving accounts About 4.15% to 4.75% p.a.

Typical bonus savings accounts at major banks were around the mid-4% range, often only if monthly conditions were met.

Home loans About 5.84% to 6.24% p.a.

Discounted owner-occupier variable home loan rates for new borrowing were commonly around the high-5% to low-6% range.

Personal loans Often 7.25% to 22.25% p.a.

Unsecured personal loans could be much higher, with representative rates around the mid-teens depending on the borrower.

Munger-Style Class Activity

A well-known investing idea often linked with Charlie Munger is that a positive base and a negative base do not just differ by the starting gap. Once compounding starts, wealth can snowball upward while debt can snowball downward, so the long-run distance can become far larger than the original difference.

Step 1. Set the starting amount to 400000, monthly contribution to 0, and choose a rate and time.
Step 2. Read the green result as the future value of +400000 invested.
Step 3. Imagine a matching -400000 debt compounding at the same rate. Its future burden has the same size but opposite sign.
Step 4. Ask students: is the gap only 800000, or is it now twice the future value after compounding?
Step 5. Change the years slider and discuss why time makes the separation explode.
