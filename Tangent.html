Applications

Then try elevation and depression in context.

4. Angle of Elevation

A student stands 24 m from a building and measures an angle of elevation of 28° to the top. Find the building height.

5. Angle of Depression

From a lookout platform, the angle of depression is 36° to a boat that is 18 m horizontally away. Find the vertical drop to the boat.

Teacher Reminders

The right angle is always the 90° corner. Mark it first before naming opposite and adjacent.
Opposite and adjacent depend on the chosen angle. They can change when the reference angle changes.
To find an angle from sides, use the inverse tangent: θ = tan-1(oppositeadjacent).
Angle of elevation looks up. Angle of depression looks down.
